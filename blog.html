Common questions about spray foam insulation in Florida

Most Florida homeowners see 30-50% reduction in energy bills immediately after spray foam installation. The average savings is $1,200-$3,200 annually, depending on home size and current insulation condition. Our customers typically see ROI within 2-3 years.

Yes, when properly installed by certified professionals, spray foam insulation is completely safe. It's non-toxic once cured, improves indoor air quality by sealing out allergens and pollutants, and has been used safely in homes for over 30 years.

Spray foam insulation lasts for many years when properly installed. Unlike traditional insulation that can settle, shift, or degrade over time, spray foam maintains its R-value and air sealing properties for the long term.

Closed-cell spray foam (R-6.5 per inch) is denser, provides structural strength, and acts as a moisture barrier - ideal for Florida's humid climate. Open-cell foam (R-3.5 per inch) is less expensive but doesn't provide moisture protection. For Florida homes, we typically recommend closed-cell for optimal performance.

Most residential projects are completed in 1-2 days. Attic insulation typically takes 4-8 hours, while whole-home projects may take 1-2 days. We work efficiently to minimize disruption to your daily routine and can often accommodate same-day installation for urgent needs.
