Why Traditional Insulation Fails in Florida Attics

1. Heat Transfer Problems

Traditional fiberglass insulation was designed for moderate climates. In Florida's extreme heat, it becomes less effective as temperatures rise, allowing more heat transfer into your home.

2. Air Leakage

Fiberglass doesn't seal air gaps around pipes, wires, and fixtures. These gaps allow hot attic air to flow directly into your living space, bypassing the insulation entirely.

3. Moisture Issues

Florida's humidity causes traditional insulation to absorb moisture, reducing its R-value by up to 50% and creating conditions for mold growth.

4. Settling and Compression

Over time, traditional insulation settles and compresses, creating gaps that allow heat transfer and reducing overall effectiveness.

The Spray Foam Solution for Florida Attics

Complete Air Sealing

Spray foam expands to fill every crack, gap, and crevice in your attic, creating a complete air barrier that prevents hot air infiltration and conditioned air loss.

Superior R-Value Performance

Closed-cell spray foam provides R-6.5 per inch compared to fiberglass's R-3.5, meaning better insulation in less space. More importantly, spray foam maintains its R-value regardless of temperature or humidity.

Moisture Control

Closed-cell spray foam is impermeable to water vapor, preventing moisture-related problems that plague traditional insulation in Florida's humid climate.

Installation Timeline and Process

Day 1: Preparation and Setup

  • Remove existing insulation (if necessary)
  • Seal electrical boxes and fixtures
  • Install proper ventilation (if needed)

Day 2: Spray Foam Application

  • Apply spray foam in multiple passes
